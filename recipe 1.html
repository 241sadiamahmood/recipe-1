Soba Noodles salad with Raw Oysters, Mushrooms and Pickled Radishes

Noodles warm up winters, but come up summers, they can transform into refreshing cooling salad, if your can't find moli, a long white japaneese radish use more round red redishes or substitue it with cute water chestnuts.Pickled sushi ginger is a great assets in the store cupboard. It can be added to almost anything even onto toast with a little miso.

Ingredients:

  • 8–10 oz soba noodles
  • 1/3 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 3 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
  • 2–3 green onions (scallions), thinly sliced.

Instructions:

  1. Bring a large pot of unsalted water to a boil. Add soba noodles and stir occasionally to prevent clumping. Cook for 4–5 minutes, or until just tender.
  2. Drain immediately and rinse the noodles vigorously under cold running water using your hands to rub off excess starch. This prevents them from clumping. Plunge in a bowl of iced water for 30 seconds to chill, then drain completely.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, and sugar until the sugar dissolves.
  4. Toss the noodles into the sauce until fully coated. Fold in the sesame seeds and green onions, and serve cold or at room temperature.
